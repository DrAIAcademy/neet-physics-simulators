Formula Explanation

These are the important AC LCR formulas from NEET point of view.

Inductive reactance: XL = 2πfL Capacitive reactance: XC = 1 / (2πfC) Impedance of series LCR: Z = √(R² + (XL − XC)²) Current: I = V / Z Phase angle: tanφ = (XL − XC) / R Power factor: cosφ = R / Z Resonance condition: XL = XC Resonant frequency: fr = 1 / (2π√LC) At resonance: Z = R, current is maximum, φ = 0, power factor = 1

NEET key: At resonance, the circuit behaves like a pure resistor.

Simple NEET Explanation

  • R opposes current by resistance.
  • L opposes change in current, so it gives inductive reactance.
  • C opposes change in voltage, so it gives capacitive reactance.
  • In AC, total opposition is called impedance.
  • If XL > XC, circuit is inductive.
  • If XC > XL, circuit is capacitive.
  • At resonance, XL and XC cancel each other.
  • So only R remains and current becomes maximum.

NEET Traps

  • At resonance, impedance is minimum, not maximum.
  • At resonance, current is maximum.
  • At resonance, power factor is 1, not 0.
  • VL and VC can be large even when net reactive part is zero.
  • XL increases with frequency.
  • XC decreases with frequency.
  • Inductive circuit means current lags voltage.
  • Capacitive circuit means current leads voltage.
