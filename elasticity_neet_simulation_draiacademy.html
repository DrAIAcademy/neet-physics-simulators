Formula Explanation

Elasticity connects force, extension and material stiffness.

Stress = Force / Area = F/AStrain = Change in length / Original length = ΔL/LYoung’s modulus: Y = Stress / StrainExtension of wire: ΔL = FL / AYHooke's law: F = kxSpring energy: U = ½kx²Area of circular wire: A = πr²Within elastic limit: stress ∝ strain

NEET key: Stress unit is N/m² or Pascal, while strain has no unit.

Simple NEET Explanation

  • Elasticity is the ability of a body to regain original shape after removing force.
  • Stress measures internal restoring force per unit area.
  • Strain measures fractional change in length.
  • Young’s modulus tells how stiff a material is.
  • Hooke’s law is valid only within elastic limit.
  • After elastic limit, material may not return fully to original shape.

NEET Traps

  • Strain has no unit.
  • Young’s modulus unit is N/m².
  • More radius means more area, so extension decreases.
  • Longer wire gives more extension for same force.
  • Hooke’s law is valid only within elastic limit.
  • Energy stored in spring is ½kx², not kx².
