Formula Explanation

Motion graphs are very important for NEET because many questions are solved by slope and area.

For x-t graph: slope = velocity For v-t graph: slope = acceleration For v-t graph: area under graph = displacement For a-t graph: area under graph = change in velocity Uniform motion: x = x₀ + vt Uniform acceleration: v = u + at Position: x = x₀ + ut + ½at² Displacement = final position − initial position

NEET key: Distance and displacement are not always the same. If direction changes, distance is greater than or equal to displacement magnitude.

Simple NEET Explanation

  • x-t graph tells how position changes with time.
  • Slope of x-t graph gives velocity.
  • v-t graph tells how velocity changes with time.
  • Slope of v-t graph gives acceleration.
  • Area under v-t graph gives displacement.
  • a-t graph tells how acceleration changes with time.
  • Area under a-t graph gives change in velocity.
  • Horizontal line in v-t graph means constant velocity.
  • Horizontal line in a-t graph means constant acceleration.

NEET Traps

  • Do not confuse slope and area.
  • Slope of x-t graph is velocity, not acceleration.
  • Area under v-t graph is displacement, not distance in all cases.
  • If velocity becomes negative, object moves in opposite direction.
  • Negative acceleration does not always mean slowing down.
  • A curved x-t graph means velocity is changing.
  • A straight v-t graph means acceleration is constant.
  • Displacement can be zero even when distance is not zero.
