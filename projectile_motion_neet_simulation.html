Formula Explanation

Projectile motion is the combination of uniform horizontal motion and accelerated vertical motion.

uₓ = u cosθ uᵧ = u sinθ x = u cosθ × t y = h + u sinθ × t − ½gt²

For level ground where initial height is zero:

Time of flight: T = 2u sinθ / g Maximum height: H = u²sin²θ / 2g Range: R = u²sin2θ / g

Important: If initial height is not zero, the simulator calculates time of flight by solving when the projectile reaches the ground.

NEET Traps

  • Horizontal velocity remains constant because there is no horizontal acceleration if air resistance is ignored.
  • Vertical velocity changes due to gravity. At the highest point, vertical velocity becomes zero.
  • Maximum range at 45° is true only for same launch and landing level.
  • Range and maximum height are different. Range is horizontal distance; height is vertical rise.
  • Acceleration is always downward and equal to g, even at the top point.

5 NEET-Style Observation Questions

  1. Keep velocity and gravity fixed. Change angle from 30° to 45° to 60°. At which angle is range maximum?
  2. Set initial height to 0 m. Compare the range at 30° and 60°. What do you observe?
  3. Increase gravity from 9.8 m/s² to 15 m/s². What happens to range and time of flight?
  4. At the highest point of trajectory, what happens to vertical velocity? What about horizontal velocity?
  5. Increase initial height. Does the projectile stay longer in air? How does range change?
