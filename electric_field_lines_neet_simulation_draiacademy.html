Formula Explanation

Electric field is a vector. Electric potential is a scalar. For multiple charges, use superposition.

Coulomb's law: F = kq1q2 / r² Magnitude: F = k|q1q2| / r² Electric field: E = F/q Point charge field: E = kq / r² Electric potential: V = kq / r Force on charge: F = qE Superposition: net field = vector sum of fields due to all charges

NEET key: Field lines come out of positive charge and enter negative charge. Field lines never intersect.

Simple NEET Explanation

  • Electric field is the region around a charge where another charge feels force.
  • Electric field lines start from positive charge and end on negative charge.
  • Field lines never intersect each other.
  • Closer field lines mean stronger electric field.
  • Electric field direction is the direction of force on a positive test charge.
  • For a negative test charge, force is opposite to electric field direction.
  • In a dipole, field lines go from positive charge to negative charge.
  • Net electric field is found using vector addition.
  • Electric potential is scalar, but electric field is vector.

NEET Traps

  • Electric field lines never cross.
  • Electric field direction is defined using a positive test charge.
  • Field lines come out of positive charge and enter negative charge.
  • Electric field is vector; electric potential is scalar.
  • At the midpoint of equal opposite charges, electric field is not zero.
  • At the midpoint of equal like charges, electric field is zero.
  • For unequal charges, neutral point is not exactly at the midpoint.
  • Electric field is stronger where field lines are closer.
  • A negative charge moves opposite to electric field direction.
  • Coulomb force follows inverse square law.
