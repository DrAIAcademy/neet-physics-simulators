Formula Explanation

Kirchhoff’s laws are used when circuits cannot be solved by simple series-parallel reduction.

Junction rule / KCL: Sum of incoming currents = Sum of outgoing currentsLoop rule / KVL: Algebraic sum of potential changes in a closed loop = 0Across resistor along current: potential change = −IRAcross resistor opposite current: potential change = +IRAcross cell from − to + terminal: potential change = +EAcross cell from + to − terminal: potential change = −EOhm’s law: V = IRAt junction: current is not consumed; charge is conserved

NEET key: Assume current directions freely. If answer is negative, actual direction is opposite.

Simple NEET Explanation

  • Kirchhoff’s junction rule is based on conservation of charge.
  • Total current entering a junction equals total current leaving it.
  • Kirchhoff’s loop rule is based on conservation of energy.
  • Total potential rise and fall in a closed loop becomes zero.
  • Battery gives potential rise when moving from negative to positive terminal.
  • Resistor gives potential drop when moving along current direction.
  • Negative current means the actual direction is opposite to assumed direction.

NEET Traps

  • Current is not used up in a resistor.
  • At a junction, incoming current equals outgoing current.
  • Do not change sign convention randomly inside the same loop.
  • Moving along current through resistor gives −IR.
  • Moving opposite current through resistor gives +IR.
  • Moving from negative to positive terminal of battery gives +E.
  • Negative answer for current only means opposite direction.
  • KVL works only for a closed loop.
