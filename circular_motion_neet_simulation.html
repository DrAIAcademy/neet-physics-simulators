Formula Explanation

Circular motion me speed constant ho sakti hai, but velocity direction change hoti hai. Direction change hone ki wajah se acceleration hota hai.

Centripetal acceleration: a = v² / r Centripetal force: F = mv² / r Angular velocity: ω = v / r Time period: T = 2πr / v Frequency: f = 1 / T Level road safe speed: vmax = √(μrg)

NEET key: Centripetal force koi new force nahi hai. It is the net inward force, provided by tension, friction, gravity, or normal reaction.

NEET Explanation

  • Speed constant ho sakti hai, lekin velocity change hoti hai because direction changes.
  • Centripetal acceleration always centre ki taraf hota hai.
  • Centripetal force is not a separate force; it is net inward force.
  • Level road par car ko turn karne ke liye friction centripetal force provide karta hai.
  • If required centripetal force is greater than available friction, vehicle skid karta hai.
  • Maximum safe speed mass par depend nahi karta because mass cancel ho jata hai.

NEET Traps

  • Centripetal force inward hota hai, outward nahi.
  • Centrifugal force non-inertial frame me pseudo force hota hai.
  • Constant speed ka matlab zero acceleration nahi hota.
  • Level road par friction centripetal force deta hai.
  • Maximum safe speed independent of mass hoti hai.
  • String break hone par object tangential direction me move karta hai, outward nahi.
