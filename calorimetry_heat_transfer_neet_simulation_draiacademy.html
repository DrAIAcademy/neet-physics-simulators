Formula Explanation

Calorimetry is based on conservation of heat energy in an insulated system.

Heat: Q = mcΔTPrinciple of calorimetry: Heat lost = Heat gainedFinal temperature: Tf = (m₁c₁T₁ + m₂c₂T₂) / (m₁c₁ + m₂c₂)Latent heat: Q = mLConduction rate: Q/t = kA(T₁ − T₂)/LThermal equilibrium: final temperatures become equalConduction needs material mediumRadiation can occur without medium

NEET key: Temperature remains constant during phase change while heat is still supplied or removed.

Simple NEET Explanation

  • Heat flows from hot body to cold body.
  • Temperature tells hotness, heat is energy transfer.
  • Specific heat tells how much heat is needed to raise temperature.
  • In mixing, final temperature lies between initial temperatures.
  • Latent heat changes phase without changing temperature.
  • Conduction happens mainly in solids.
  • Convection happens mainly in fluids.
  • Radiation does not need a medium.

NEET Traps

  • Do not confuse heat and temperature.
  • Final temperature in mixing lies between hot and cold temperatures.
  • Use kg, not gram, in SI calculations.
  • During melting/boiling, temperature remains constant.
  • Latent heat formula is Q = mL, not mcΔT.
  • Radiation can travel through vacuum.
  • Conduction rate increases with area and temperature difference.
  • Conduction rate decreases when rod length increases.
