Formula Explanation

Spring-mass SHM happens when restoring force is directly proportional to displacement and always points towards mean position.

SHM condition: F = −kx Acceleration relation: a = −ω²x Displacement: x = A sin(ωt + φ) Angular frequency: ω = √(k/m) Time period: T = 2π√(m/k) Frequency: f = 1/T Total energy: E = ½kA²

NEET key: In ideal SHM, time period depends on mass and spring constant, not on amplitude.

Simple NEET Explanation

  • SHM is repeated to-and-fro motion about the mean position.
  • Restoring force always acts towards mean position.
  • At mean position, speed is maximum and acceleration is zero.
  • At extreme position, speed is zero and acceleration is maximum.
  • Increasing mass increases time period.
  • Increasing spring constant decreases time period.
  • For ideal SHM, time period does not depend on amplitude.

NEET Traps

  • At mean position, acceleration is zero but velocity is maximum.
  • At extreme position, velocity is zero but acceleration is maximum.
  • Restoring force is always opposite to displacement.
  • SHM acceleration is not constant; it changes with displacement.
  • Time period of ideal spring-mass SHM does not depend on amplitude.
  • Total energy remains constant in ideal SHM.
  • Kinetic energy is maximum at mean position.
  • Potential energy is maximum at extreme positions.
