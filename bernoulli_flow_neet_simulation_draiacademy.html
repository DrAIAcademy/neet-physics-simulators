Formula Explanation

Bernoulli's theorem is conservation of mechanical energy per unit volume for ideal fluid flow.

Bernoulli equation: P + ½ρv² + ρgh = constant Continuity equation: A1v1 = A2v2 Speed at section 2: v2 = A1v1 / A2 Pressure change: P2 − P1 = ½ρ(v1² − v2²) + ρg(h1 − h2) Dynamic pressure: ½ρv² For same height: faster flow → lower pressure

NEET key: Bernoulli applies to ideal, incompressible, non-viscous, streamline flow.

Simple NEET Explanation

  • Bernoulli theorem connects pressure energy, kinetic energy, and potential energy of a flowing fluid.
  • When pipe becomes narrow, area decreases and speed increases by continuity equation.
  • If speed increases at the same height, pressure decreases.
  • Venturimeter works on pressure difference between wide and narrow parts of a tube.
  • Atomizer works because fast air has lower pressure and pulls liquid upward.
  • Airfoil lift concept: faster air above wing creates lower pressure above.
  • Bernoulli equation is valid for ideal fluid and streamline flow.

NEET Traps

  • Fast flow means low pressure only when height is same or height effect is considered properly.
  • Continuity equation says Av is constant for incompressible steady flow.
  • Narrow pipe does not reduce flow rate in ideal steady flow; it increases speed.
  • Bernoulli equation is not directly valid for turbulent or highly viscous flow.
  • Pressure is not always highest where height is highest.
  • Dynamic pressure term is ½ρv², not ρv².
  • ρgh term must be included when height changes.
  • Venturi effect: narrow section has greater velocity and lower pressure.
