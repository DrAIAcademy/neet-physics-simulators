Formula Explanation

Bohr model gives direct formulas for energy levels, orbit radius, and spectral wavelengths.

Energy level of hydrogen-like atom: Eₙ = −13.6 Z² / n² eVBohr radius: rₙ = 0.529 n² / Z ÅPhoton energy: ΔE = Eᵢ − E𝒇 = hf = hc/λRydberg formula: 1/λ = RZ²(1/n𝒇² − 1/nᵢ²)Lyman series: n𝒇 = 1Balmer series: n𝒇 = 2Paschen series: n𝒇 = 3Emission: electron jumps from higher level to lower level

NEET key: Energy levels are negative because the electron is bound to the nucleus.

Simple NEET Explanation

  • Bohr said electron can revolve only in allowed orbits.
  • Each orbit has fixed energy.
  • Electron does not radiate energy while staying in a stationary orbit.
  • When electron jumps down, photon is emitted.
  • When electron jumps up, photon is absorbed.
  • Different transitions produce different spectral lines.
  • Balmer series ends at n = 2 and is important for visible hydrogen spectrum.

NEET Traps

  • Energy of bound electron is negative.
  • Ionization energy from n = 1 for hydrogen is 13.6 eV.
  • Emission occurs from higher n to lower n.
  • Absorption occurs from lower n to higher n.
  • Lyman series ends at n = 1.
  • Balmer series ends at n = 2.
  • Radius is proportional to n².
  • Energy magnitude decreases as n increases.
