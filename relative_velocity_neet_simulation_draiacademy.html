Formula Explanation

Relative velocity problems become easy when every velocity is broken into components.

Relative velocity: vAB = vA − vB River-boat ground velocity: vBG = vBR + vRG Time to cross river: t = width / perpendicular component of boat velocity Drift = river speed × crossing time Rain umbrella angle: tanθ = horizontal velocity / vertical rain velocity Same direction relative speed: |v1 − v2| Opposite direction relative speed: v1 + v2

NEET key: Do not add or subtract speeds blindly. Use vector direction carefully.

Simple NEET Explanation

  • Relative velocity means how fast one object appears to move from another object's point of view.
  • Velocity is a vector, so direction matters.
  • In river-boat problems, boat velocity relative to water and river velocity combine to give ground velocity.
  • For shortest time across river, boat should point perpendicular to river bank.
  • For zero drift, boat should be aimed upstream at a suitable angle.
  • In rain problems, umbrella is tilted opposite to apparent rain direction.
  • For two objects moving in same direction, relative speed is difference of speeds.
  • For opposite direction, relative speed is sum of speeds.

NEET Traps

  • Relative velocity is vector subtraction, not simple arithmetic in all cases.
  • Shortest time and shortest path in river crossing are different conditions.
  • River current affects drift but not perpendicular crossing component.
  • If boat speed is less than river speed, zero drift may not be possible.
  • Apparent rain direction depends on relative velocity of rain with respect to man.
  • Opposite direction relative speed is sum, same direction is difference.
  • Angle should be calculated using correct components.
  • Velocity has direction; speed does not.
