Formula Explanation

Resolve weight mg into two components because the surface is inclined.

Component down the plane = mg sinθ Component perpendicular to plane = mg cosθ Normal reaction: N = mg cosθ Maximum friction: f = μN = μmg cosθ

If mg sinθ ≤ μmg cosθ, the block stays at rest because friction can balance the downward component.

If mg sinθ > μmg cosθ, the block slides down and acceleration is:

a = g(sinθ − μ cosθ)

NEET trap: Mass cancels in acceleration. On an inclined plane, normal reaction is not mg; it is mg cosθ.

5 NEET-Style Observation Questions

  1. Keep mass fixed and slowly increase the angle. At what angle does the block start sliding for μ = 0.30?
  2. Increase the mass from 5 kg to 15 kg. Does the acceleration change after the block starts sliding? Why?
  3. For θ = 30°, increase μ. What value of μ is just enough to stop the block from sliding?
  4. When θ increases, what happens to mg sinθ and mg cosθ?
  5. Why is friction upward along the plane when the block tends to slide downward?
