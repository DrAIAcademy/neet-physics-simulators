Formula Explanation

Magnetic force acts only when charge is moving or when current flows in a conductor.

Force on moving charge: F = qvB sinθ Force magnitude: |F| = |q|vB sinθ Force on conductor: F = BIL sinθ Circular path radius: r = mv / |q|B Time period: T = 2πm / |q|B Angular frequency: ω = |q|B / m Magnetic force is perpendicular to velocity and magnetic field. Magnetic force does no work because it is perpendicular to velocity.

NEET key: For negative charge, magnetic force direction is opposite to the right-hand rule direction.

Simple NEET Explanation

  • A moving charge experiences force in a magnetic field.
  • A charge at rest does not experience magnetic force.
  • Force is maximum when velocity is perpendicular to magnetic field.
  • Force is zero when velocity is parallel or anti-parallel to magnetic field.
  • For positive charge, direction is found by right-hand rule.
  • For negative charge, force direction is opposite.
  • When velocity is perpendicular to B, magnetic force provides centripetal force.
  • Magnetic force changes direction of velocity, not speed.
  • For a current-carrying conductor, force depends on B, I, L, and angle.

NEET Traps

  • Magnetic force acts only on moving charge, not on charge at rest.
  • Magnetic force is maximum at θ = 90°.
  • Magnetic force is zero at θ = 0° or 180°.
  • Magnetic force does no work.
  • Magnetic force changes direction of velocity, not speed.
  • For negative charge, force direction is opposite to right-hand rule direction.
  • In circular motion, magnetic force provides centripetal force.
  • Radius increases when mass or velocity increases.
  • Radius decreases when charge or magnetic field increases.
  • Time period does not depend on velocity.
  • Dots mean B coming out of screen. Crosses mean B going into screen.
