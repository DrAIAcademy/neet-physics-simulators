Formula Explanation

Photoelectric effect proves the particle nature of light and is explained by Einstein's equation.

Photon energy: E = hfWork function: φ = hf₀Einstein photoelectric equation: Kmax = hf − φThreshold condition: f ≥ f₀Stopping potential: eVs = KmaxKmax in eV = Vs in voltIntensity controls number of photoelectrons/currentFrequency controls maximum kinetic energy

NEET key: No emission occurs below threshold frequency, no matter how high intensity is.

Simple NEET Explanation

  • Light is made of photons.
  • Each photon has energy E = hf.
  • Electron needs minimum energy called work function to escape metal.
  • If photon energy is less than work function, no electron is emitted.
  • If frequency increases, maximum kinetic energy increases.
  • If intensity increases, number of emitted electrons increases.
  • Stopping potential is used to stop fastest photoelectrons.

NEET Traps

  • High intensity cannot cause emission below threshold frequency.
  • Intensity affects current, not maximum kinetic energy.
  • Frequency affects maximum kinetic energy.
  • Threshold frequency depends on metal.
  • Stopping potential depends on frequency, not intensity.
  • Photoelectric emission is almost instantaneous.
  • Work function is measured in eV or joule.
  • Einstein equation is Kmax = hf − φ.
