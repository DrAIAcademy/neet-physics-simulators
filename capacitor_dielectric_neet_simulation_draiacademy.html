Formula Explanation

Capacitors are easy when you know what remains constant: voltage or charge.

Parallel plate capacitor: C = ε₀A/dWith dielectric fully inserted: C' = KCCharge stored: Q = CVElectric field: E = V/dEnergy stored: U = ½CV² = Q²/2C = ½QVSeries capacitors: 1/Ceq = 1/C₁ + 1/C₂Parallel capacitors: Ceq = C₁ + C₂Battery connected: V constant. Battery removed: Q constant.

NEET key: Inserting dielectric increases capacitance. What happens to V, Q and U depends on whether battery is connected or removed.

Simple NEET Explanation

  • A capacitor stores charge on two conducting plates separated by an insulator.
  • Capacitance increases when plate area increases.
  • Capacitance decreases when plate separation increases.
  • Dielectric increases capacitance by reducing effective electric field.
  • With battery connected, voltage remains fixed and charge increases.
  • With battery removed, charge remains fixed and voltage decreases.
  • In series, same charge flows on each capacitor.
  • In parallel, same voltage appears across each capacitor.

NEET Traps

  • Do not assume both charge and voltage remain constant.
  • Battery connected means voltage constant.
  • Battery removed means charge constant.
  • Dielectric increases capacitance.
  • In series, equivalent capacitance is less than smallest capacitor.
  • In parallel, equivalent capacitance is sum of capacitors.
  • Electric field between plates is E = V/d.
  • Energy may increase or decrease after dielectric insertion depending on condition.

NEET-Style Observation Questions

  1. What happens to capacitance when plate area increases?
  2. What happens to capacitance when plate separation increases?
  3. What remains constant when dielectric is inserted with battery connected?
  4. What remains constant when dielectric is inserted after battery is removed?
  5. Why does dielectric increase capacitance?
  6. What is equivalent capacitance in parallel?
  7. Which is smaller in series: equivalent capacitance or smallest individual capacitance?
