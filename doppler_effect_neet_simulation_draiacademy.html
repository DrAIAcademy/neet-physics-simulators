Formula Explanation

Doppler effect questions are solved by choosing the correct sign for observer and source motion.

General sound Doppler formula: f' = f × (v ± vo) / (v ∓ vs)Observer moving towards source: use v + voObserver moving away from source: use v − voSource moving towards observer: use v − vs in denominatorSource moving away from observer: use v + vs in denominatorSource moving towards observer: wavelength in front decreasesSource moving away: wavelength behind increasesShock wave condition: source speed greater than sound speed

NEET key: Pitch increases when apparent frequency increases. Frequency of source does not actually change.

Simple NEET Explanation

  • Doppler effect is change in apparent frequency due to relative motion.
  • If source and observer approach, apparent frequency increases.
  • If source and observer move away, apparent frequency decreases.
  • Moving source changes wavelength in front and behind.
  • Moving observer receives wavefronts faster or slower.
  • Sound Doppler effect needs medium, so source and observer speeds are taken relative to medium.
  • If source speed exceeds sound speed, shock wave can form.

NEET Traps

  • Source frequency remains same; apparent frequency changes.
  • Moving source changes wavelength; moving observer changes rate of receiving waves.
  • Use correct signs carefully.
  • Approaching means higher apparent frequency.
  • Receding means lower apparent frequency.
  • Doppler formula for sound is different from light.
  • Speed of sound is relative to medium.
  • Supersonic source can create shock wave/Mach cone.
