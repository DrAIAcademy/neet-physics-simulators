Formula Explanation

Thermodynamics process questions become easy by using first law and the P-V graph.

First law: Q = ΔU + WIdeal gas equation: PV = nRTIsothermal: T constant, ΔU = 0, W = nRT ln(V₂/V₁)Adiabatic: Q = 0, PVγ = constantIsobaric: P constant, W = P(V₂ − V₁)Isochoric: V constant, W = 0For ideal gas: ΔU = nCvΔTWork done by gas = area under P-V curve

NEET key: Isothermal has ΔU = 0, adiabatic has Q = 0, isochoric has W = 0.

Simple NEET Explanation

  • Thermodynamics studies heat, work and internal energy.
  • In isothermal process, temperature remains constant.
  • In adiabatic process, heat exchange is zero.
  • In isobaric process, pressure remains constant.
  • In isochoric process, volume remains constant.
  • Area under P-V graph gives work done by gas.
  • Expansion gives positive work; compression gives negative work.
  • Internal energy of ideal gas depends only on temperature.

NEET Traps

  • Do not confuse isothermal and adiabatic curves.
  • Adiabatic curve is steeper than isothermal curve.
  • Isochoric process has zero work because volume does not change.
  • Isothermal ideal gas has ΔU = 0.
  • Adiabatic process has Q = 0, not W = 0.
  • Work is area under P-V graph, not slope.
  • Expansion work is positive by gas convention.
  • Use volume in m³, not litre, in SI calculations.
