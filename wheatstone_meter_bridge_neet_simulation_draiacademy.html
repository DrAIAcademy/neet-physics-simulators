Formula Explanation

Wheatstone bridge and meter bridge are null-deflection methods for comparing resistances.

Wheatstone balance condition: P/Q = R/XAt balance: Ig = 0Unknown resistance: X = QR/PMeter bridge balance: R/X = l/(100 − l)Unknown in meter bridge: X = R(100 − l)/lAt null point, galvanometer carries no currentMeter bridge wire has uniform resistance per unit lengthBest balance length is near middle, around 40 cm to 60 cm

NEET key: Meter bridge is a practical form of Wheatstone bridge using length ratio instead of resistance ratio.

Simple NEET Explanation

  • Wheatstone bridge compares four resistances.
  • When bridge is balanced, galvanometer current becomes zero.
  • Balance condition is P/Q = R/X.
  • Meter bridge uses a 1 metre uniform wire.
  • In meter bridge, resistance is proportional to length of wire.
  • Jockey is moved until galvanometer shows null deflection.
  • Null method is accurate because it does not depend on galvanometer calibration.

NEET Traps

  • At balance, galvanometer current is zero, not maximum.
  • Meter bridge uses length ratio because resistance ∝ length.
  • Use l and 100 − l correctly.
  • Do not reverse R and X in formula.
  • Best null point is near middle of wire.
  • End correction can affect practical readings.
  • Wheatstone bridge is not used to measure very low resistance accurately.
  • At balance, battery current still flows in outer arms.
