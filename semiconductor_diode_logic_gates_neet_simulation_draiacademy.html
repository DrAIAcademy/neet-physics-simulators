NEET key: In forward bias, current increases sharply after cut-in voltage. In reverse bias, current remains small until breakdown.

Simple NEET Explanation

  • A semiconductor has conductivity between conductor and insulator.
  • P-type semiconductor has holes as majority carriers.
  • N-type semiconductor has electrons as majority carriers.
  • When P and N join, depletion region forms at junction.
  • Forward bias reduces depletion region and allows current.
  • Reverse bias increases depletion region and blocks current.
  • LED emits light in forward bias.
  • Photodiode is generally used in reverse bias.
  • Logic gates perform Boolean operations using binary inputs.

NEET Traps

  • P-type does not mean positively charged; it is electrically neutral overall.
  • N-type does not mean negatively charged; it is electrically neutral overall.
  • Forward bias reduces barrier potential.
  • Reverse bias increases depletion width.
  • Ordinary diode should not operate in reverse breakdown.
  • Zener diode is designed to work in reverse breakdown.
  • LED works in forward bias.
  • Photodiode usually works in reverse bias.
  • NAND and NOR are universal gates.
