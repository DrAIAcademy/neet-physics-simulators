Formula Explanation

Interference happens when two coherent waves meet and combine. Resultant intensity depends on phase difference.

Wave speed relation: v = fλ Path difference: Δx = |S2P − S1P| Phase difference: Δφ = 2πΔx / λ Constructive interference: Δx = nλ Destructive interference: Δx = (n + 1/2)λ For equal amplitudes: R = 2A cos(Δφ/2) Intensity relation: I ∝ A²

NEET key: Bright fringe means maximum resultant intensity. Dark fringe means cancellation due to phase opposition.

Simple NEET Explanation

  • Interference happens when two waves meet and combine.
  • Constructive interference means waves add and intensity becomes maximum.
  • Destructive interference means waves cancel and intensity becomes minimum.
  • Coherent sources have the same frequency and constant phase difference.
  • Bright fringes form where path difference is nλ.
  • Dark fringes form where path difference is (n + 1/2)λ.
  • If wavelength increases, fringe spacing increases.
  • If source distance increases, fringe spacing decreases.

NEET Traps

  • Interference needs coherent sources.
  • Bright fringe means resultant intensity is maximum, not that only one wave is bright.
  • Dark fringe does not mean no wave reaches there; waves reach but cancel.
  • Path difference and phase difference are related but not the same.
  • Intensity is proportional to square of amplitude, not directly amplitude.
  • Constructive condition is Δx = nλ.
  • Destructive condition is Δx = (n + 1/2)λ.
  • Increasing wavelength increases fringe width.
  • Increasing source separation decreases fringe width.
  • Coherent sources must have constant phase difference.
