Formula Explanation

Satellite motion is an application of gravitation and circular motion together.

Gravitational force: F = GMm/r²Orbital velocity: v = √(GM/r)Escape velocity: ve = √(2GM/R)Time period: T = 2π√(r³/GM)Acceleration due to gravity at height: g' = GM/r²Total satellite energy: E = −GMm/2rPotential energy: U = −GMm/rKinetic energy in orbit: K = GMm/2r

NEET key: Satellite mass cancels in orbital velocity and time period formulas.

Simple NEET Explanation

  • Every mass attracts every other mass by gravitational force.
  • A satellite stays in orbit because gravity provides centripetal force.
  • Orbital velocity decreases when orbital radius increases.
  • Escape velocity is √2 times orbital velocity near the surface.
  • Satellite total energy is negative for a bound orbit.
  • Geostationary satellite has time period of about 24 hours.
  • Satellite mass does not affect orbital speed.

NEET Traps

  • Use r = R + h, not only h.
  • Orbital velocity is independent of satellite mass.
  • Time period increases strongly with orbital radius.
  • Escape velocity depends on planet, not projectile mass.
  • Total energy of bound satellite is negative.
  • Potential energy is twice total energy: U = 2E.
  • Kinetic energy is positive but total energy is negative.
  • Geostationary orbit needs equatorial plane and 24 hour period.
