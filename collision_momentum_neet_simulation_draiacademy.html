Formula Explanation

Collision problems are solved using conservation of momentum and coefficient of restitution.

Momentum: p = mv Conservation of momentum: m₁u₁ + m₂u₂ = m₁v₁ + m₂v₂ Coefficient of restitution: e = (v₂ − v₁) / (u₁ − u₂) Perfect elastic collision: e = 1 and kinetic energy is conserved Perfect inelastic collision: e = 0 and bodies move together Kinetic energy: K = ½mv² Impulse: J = Δp = change in momentum Explosion: initial momentum = final momentum

NEET key: Momentum is vector quantity. Direction/sign of velocity must be taken carefully.

Simple NEET Explanation

  • Momentum means quantity of motion: p = mv.
  • In collision, total momentum remains conserved if external force is zero.
  • Elastic collision conserves both momentum and kinetic energy.
  • Inelastic collision conserves momentum but kinetic energy decreases.
  • Perfectly inelastic collision means bodies stick together after collision.
  • Coefficient of restitution tells how bouncy a collision is.
  • Impulse is change in momentum.
  • Velocity signs are very important in collision problems.

NEET Traps

  • Momentum is conserved, kinetic energy may not be conserved.
  • Kinetic energy is conserved only in perfectly elastic collision.
  • Do not ignore negative sign of velocity.
  • Perfectly inelastic means both bodies have same final velocity.
  • Coefficient of restitution lies between 0 and 1 for normal cases.
  • Explosion also follows momentum conservation.
  • Momentum is vector; kinetic energy is scalar.
  • Equal and opposite impulses act on colliding bodies.
