Formula Explanation

Work-energy theorem is one of the fastest methods to solve NEET mechanics questions.

Work done: W = Fs cosθ Kinetic energy: K = ½mv² Work-energy theorem: Wnet = ΔK = Kf − Ki Gravitational potential energy: U = mgh Spring potential energy: Us = ½kx² Friction work: Wf = −fs If only conservative forces act: Mechanical energy remains constant If friction acts: Mechanical energy decreases by work done against friction

NEET key: Work can be positive, negative, or zero depending on angle between force and displacement.

Simple NEET Explanation

  • Work is energy transferred by force through displacement.
  • If force and displacement are in the same direction, work is positive.
  • If force is opposite to displacement, work is negative.
  • If force is perpendicular to displacement, work is zero.
  • Net work done changes kinetic energy.
  • Friction does negative work and reduces mechanical energy.
  • Gravity can convert potential energy into kinetic energy.
  • Spring stores energy as ½kx² when compressed or stretched.

NEET Traps

  • Work done by normal reaction is zero when displacement is along surface.
  • Friction usually does negative work.
  • Net work, not only applied work, equals change in kinetic energy.
  • Work is scalar, but force and displacement are vectors.
  • Positive work increases kinetic energy; negative net work decreases kinetic energy.
  • Mechanical energy is conserved only when non-conservative forces are absent.
  • Spring energy depends on x², so compression and extension both store positive energy.
  • Use height h in mgh, not path length unless motion is vertical.
