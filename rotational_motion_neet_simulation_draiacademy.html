Formula Explanation

Rotational motion is similar to linear motion, but variables change from x, v, a, F to θ, ω, α, τ.

Torque: τ = rFsinθRotational Newton's law: τ = IαAngular velocity: ω = v/RRotational kinetic energy: K = ½Iω²Angular momentum: L = IωRolling without slipping: v = RωTotal KE in rolling: K = ½mv² + ½Iω²If no external torque acts: angular momentum is conserved

NEET key: More moment of inertia means less angular acceleration for the same torque.

Simple NEET Explanation

  • Torque is turning effect of force.
  • Force applied farther from axis produces more torque.
  • Torque is maximum when force is perpendicular to radius.
  • Moment of inertia measures resistance to rotational motion.
  • Ring has greater moment of inertia than solid disk for same mass and radius.
  • Rolling without slipping means v = Rω.
  • Angular momentum remains constant when external torque is zero.

NEET Traps

  • Torque is not simply rF; it is rFsinθ.
  • Force through centre gives zero torque.
  • Moment of inertia depends on axis of rotation.
  • Rolling kinetic energy has both translational and rotational parts.
  • Angular momentum is vector, but NEET often uses magnitude.
  • Conservation of angular momentum needs zero external torque.
  • Ring and disk with same mass and radius do not have same I.
  • Angular acceleration decreases when moment of inertia increases.

  4. What is the condition for rolling without slipping?
